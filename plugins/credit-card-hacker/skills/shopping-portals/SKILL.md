# shopping-portals — stack on top of card earnings

## When to load

- User mentions a specific online purchase (Apple, Best Buy, Macy's, etc.)
- Building an MSR plan that includes online shopping
- User asks about Rakuten, TopCashback, etc.
- Black Friday / Cyber Monday planning

## The four major portal types

### Cashback portals (paid in $)
- **Rakuten** — largest. Pays in cash OR Amex MR (link Amex; rates often higher in MR mode for Plat/Gold/Green holders)
- **TopCashback** — best base rates, pay-in-cash-only
- **Capital One Shopping** — built into browser extension; auto-applies coupons + portal cashback. Cash-only.

### Card-issuer offers (paid in card rewards)
- **Amex Offers** — load specific offers in Amex app, spend at merchant, get statement credit. Often $20 off $100 type deals. Stack with Rakuten when possible.
- **Chase Offers** — similar in Chase app
- **BoA Deals** — similar
- **Citi Merchant Offers** — similar

### Airline portals (paid in miles)
- **United MileagePlus X** (mobile app) — mile bonuses on dining + retail
- **AAdvantage eShopping** — mile bonuses on online retail
- **Alaska Mileage Plan Shopping** — mile bonuses
- **Delta SkyMiles Shopping**

### Hotel portals
- **Hilton Shopping (Hilton Honors Shopping Mall)** — small Hilton Honors bonuses

## How to stack

For an Apple purchase:
1. Card: Apple Pay via CSR (Apple TV+ credit, but limited stack) or Custom Cash if "online shopping" is your top
2. Portal: Rakuten currently 1% (varies, sometimes 4–6% during Apple promos) — paid in MR if Amex card linked
3. Amex Offer: occasional "$25 off $250 at Apple" — stack
4. Apple gift card preload: buy Apple GC at grocery (5x cat) or rotator → use GC at Apple

Net effective return can hit 8–12% on a single transaction with all stacks.

## When portals are not worth it

- Same-day urgent purchases (portals require fresh browser session, click through to merchant; closing tabs invalidates)
- Recurring subscriptions (one-time portal click only credits the first transaction)
- Mobile-app-only purchases (some merchants don't track from mobile)

## Tracking realization

Portals credit 30–90 days after the transaction. Some merchants explicitly exclude (Apple, Best Buy on certain product lines). Track in a spreadsheet OR use Cashback Monitor (cashbackmonitor.com) which compares all portal rates.

## Realistic value

Average user nets $200–500/year from portals on existing online spend. Not transformative. Worth doing for users who already shop online; not worth onboarding a non-shopper to.
