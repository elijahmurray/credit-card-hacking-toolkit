# business-card-strategy — sole prop is enough

## The threshold

You qualify as a "business" for credit card application purposes if you have ANY income-generating activity, even informal:

- Freelance / consulting / 1099 income (any amount, even <$500/yr)
- eBay, Etsy, Mercari, Poshmark seller account
- Uber / DoorDash / Instacart driver
- Rental property
- YouTube / Patreon / OnlyFans / Substack (any monetized content)
- Tutoring, dog walking, lawn care — paid in cash counts
- Amazon affiliate, blog, podcast with sponsors
- Plans to start any of the above

Issuers do not require an EIN, formal LLC filing, or business bank account. SSN + "sole proprietorship" + your name as business name + a plausible business type is enough.

## When to load

- Recommending any business card (Chase Ink, Amex Business Plat/Gold, Citi Business, BoA Business)
- User says "I don't have a business" — check for side hustle
- Planning Ink rotation (multiple Ink apps over 12mo)
- Player-2 strategy includes business apps

## Filling out the application

### Business name
- Your full legal name ("John Smith") — most common for sole prop
- OR DBA / business name if you have one ("John's Etsy Shop")

### Business type
- Most issuers offer dropdown. Pick the closest:
  - "Sole Proprietorship" — default
  - "LLC" — only if you actually formed one
  - "Other"

### Tax ID
- **SSN** — required for sole prop. Same SSN on personal and business apps.
- **EIN** — optional but recommended if available. Free to get at irs.gov (5min). Doesn't change credit reporting; just keeps SSN off the business card directly.

### Years in business / months in business
- Be honest. "1 year" or "<1 year" is fine. Issuers do not auto-deny low-tenure businesses.

### Annual revenue
- Use ACTUAL revenue from the side hustle. If you made $200 on eBay last year, put $200. Don't inflate.
- If revenue is $0 ("just starting"), that's also fine — issuers approve plenty of $0-revenue sole props.

### Number of employees
- 1 (yourself)

### Business address
- Home address is fine for sole prop

## 5/24 implications by issuer

| Issuer | Business cards count toward 5/24? |
|---|---|
| **Chase** | NO (don't report to personal bureaus) |
| **Amex** | NO |
| **Citi** | NO |
| **BoA** | NO |
| **US Bank** | NO |
| **Wells Fargo** | NO |
| **Capital One (Spark)** | YES — Spark reports to personal |
| **Discover Business** | YES — reports to personal |

**Important:** even though Chase business cards don't COUNT toward 5/24, Chase still CHECKS your 5/24 when approving. If you're at 5/24, you can't get an Ink either.

## Ink rotation strategy

The Chase Ink family is the highest-floor public play in churning because:
- No family-level lockout (each Ink is a separate 24-month per-product clock)
- High SUBs ($750–$1500 per card)
- Don't count toward 5/24
- Easy MSR ($6K–$8K, often coverable by tax payments)

**Standard rotation (P1 alone, ~1 Ink per quarter):**
- Q1: Ink Business Cash → SUB earned by Q2
- Q2: Ink Business Unlimited → SUB by Q3
- Q3: Ink Business Preferred → SUB by Q4
- Q4: Ink Business Premier (or repeat Cash if past 24mo)

**With P2: 8 Inks per year possible** (4 each, staggered). See `player-2-strategy`.

## Risk flags for business apps

- Chase Ink applications close together with same business name + EIN → manual review (`velocity-guardrails`)
- Inflating revenue → grounds for closure on financial review
- Listing your business as something it's not (saying "Real Estate" when you sell on Etsy) → closure risk
- Applying for business cards with $0 personal income → some issuers auto-deny (Cap One Spark especially)

## What if user genuinely has no business activity?

Then they don't qualify and should not apply. Don't suggest fabricating a business — that's misrepresentation on a credit application (federal crime in extreme cases, account closure in normal cases).

Suggest they consider whether they have ANY of the activities listed above. Most users do — they just didn't realize it counts.
